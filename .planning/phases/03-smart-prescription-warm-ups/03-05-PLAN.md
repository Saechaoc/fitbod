<interfaces>
ProgressionStrategy protocol (from RESEARCH §Code Examples, normative):

- protocol ProgressionStrategy: Sendable
- single requirement: `func prescribe(history: [HistoryPoint], targetRepsLow: Int, targetRepsHigh: Int, targetRPE: Double?, smallestIncrement: Double, plates: [(weight: Double, countPerSide: Int)], barWeight: Double, minCalibrationSets: Int, lastSessionWeight: Double?, lastSessionReps: Int?, lastSessionRPE: Double?, lastSessionDate: Date?) -> (weight: Double, explanation: PrescriptionExplanation)`

The HistoryPoint type was shipped in plan 03-03. CalibrationStatus and PrescriptionExplanation ship in this plan.

WarmupRamp percentages (CONTEXT.md Area 3 verbatim):
- Barbell: 40% × 5, 60% × 3, 75% × 2, 90% × 1 of top working weight
- Dumbbell: 60% × 3, 90% × 1 (halved set count for unilateral)

WarmupRamp guards (CONTEXT.md Area 3 + UI-SPEC § Warm-up ramp insertion flow):
- mechanic must be .compound
- equipment must be .barbell OR .dumbbell
- working weight must be >= 1.5 * barWeight (else skip)
- bodyweight equipment: skip
- deload flag true: skip
- warmupOverride.enabled == false: skip

ProgressionKind cases (existing): .rpe (raw "rpe"), .double (raw "double"), .block, .hybrid. Per UI-SPEC § What's Explicitly Deferred, .block and .hybrid "silently behave like Double Progression in Phase 3 sessions" — factory MUST route them to DoubleProgressionStrategy.
